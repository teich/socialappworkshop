Social App Workshop: Learn! Code! Compete!
Social App Workshop (@appsmackdown) is a hacker event for new and experienced developers working on Twitter & Facebook apps. Experienced coders can show off their social apps while new app developers will learn from the best. The event will include demos, hack-a-thon with hands-on training and an apps contest at the end of the day. Organized by Heroku, Apigee and a supporting cast of app developers, VCs and media, you won't want to miss this opportunity to show off your app or learn how to create "Apps that Smack".

Speaker Bios
Intro to Social AppDev (Oren Teich of Heroku)
Oren has worked to make developers lives better for 12 years. From the first server appliance company, Cobalt Networks, through embedded company MontaVista, dearly departed Sun and now Heroku, Oren has been working in product, engineering and marketing to make sure developers don't hate. Oren is currently responsible for product at Heroku, where he tries daily to live up to the standards of simplicity and elegance that the founders scream about every day.
Demo (Ari Newman, founder of Filtrbox, Newman Venture Advisors)
Ari got the startup bug early, and hasn't looked back since working for his first startup while in college. Most recently, he was the founder and President of Filtrbox (now Jive Software). Ari loves working in (and on) early stage companies and digs the business side as much as the technical. Ari's a bay area native & ex-pat who now calls Boulder, CO home.
Intro to Social APIs (Sam Ramji, Apigee)
Sam's spent over 15 years working across enterprise software, product development and open source strategy. Currently VP of strategy at Apigee & board member at the CodePlex Foundation, Sam works deeply within the developer, open source and social app communities to create a healthier, more productive ecosystem. Prior to Apigee, Sam led open source strategy across Microsoft and was a founding member of the AquaLogic product team at BEA. In his spare time, Sam enjoys the great outdoors, spending time with the kids, and bacon.
Speaker (Matt Harris, Twitter)
Matt, originally from England, now calls San Francisco home and is one of the Developer Advocates at Twitter. He can be found at BarCamps, Meetups and HackCamps between the two hemispheres helping developers and users build upon the Twitter API. A self confessed gadget guy who loves his Aibo (robot dog) and makes a delicious Beef Wellington.
VC Panel (Harjeet Taggar, Venture Partner, Y Combinator)
Harjeet is currently Venture Partner at Y Combinator. Prior to this he was the co-founder of Auctomatic, a YC funded company in 2007 building online sales software, which was acquired by Live Current Media in 2008. Prior to moving to Silicon Valley, Harjeet studied at Oxford and originally hails from London.
VC Panel (David Lee, SV Angel)
David Lee is an angel investor at SV Angel, an angel fund that invests in consumer Internet, mobile and other high-tech companies. Some representative investments include Stack Overflow, Hunch, Dailybooth, Nowmov and Foursquare. Prior to SV Angel, David spent time at Google, StumbleUpon and Baseline Ventures.
VC Panel (Steve Anderson, Baseline Ventures)
A Seattle native, Steve Anderson is a seed investor. Steve founded Baseline Ventures, a privately held early stage seed investor in 2006. Bringing his diverse experience from eBay, Microsoft, Kleiner Perkins, Starbucks and Digital Equipment Corporation, Steve founded Baseline to in order to help entrepreneuers build and grow their ideas into companies.
